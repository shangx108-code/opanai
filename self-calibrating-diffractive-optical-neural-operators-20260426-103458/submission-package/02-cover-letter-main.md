# Cover Letter Main

Dear Editors,

Please consider our manuscript, **Self-calibrating diffractive optical neural operators for imaging through dynamic aberrations**, for publication in *Nature Communications*.

Our manuscript advances a mechanism-first contribution in diffractive optical computing: we show that a common-path optical pilot can encode information about the instantaneous degradation state and thereby improve downstream imaging recovery under dynamic aberrations. The strongest evidence comes from the wave-optics and task-level analyses, which remain consistently aligned with this bounded claim. We also retain a processor-level passive diffractive comparison, not as a mature device demonstration, but as transparent limit-setting evidence showing where the present implementation remains weak. We believe this evidence-bounded framing is important for the field because it distinguishes a real conditional-optics mechanism from an overstated device-level claim.

Figures 1--4 carry the main positive evidence chain by establishing the mechanism, its wave-optics persistence, the pilot-information diagnosis, and the task-dependent downstream behavior. Figure 5 is kept deliberately as processor-level boundary evidence. It no longer leaves the device-level comparison absent, but it also does not claim a robust ordinary-beating passive implementation. We regard that transparency as part of the manuscript's value.

The package's current Figure 5 wording is intentionally conservative and internally consistent across the manuscript, reviewer response, and the compact quantitative insert. We do not present the boundary chain as a successful passive-device restoration result, and we do not convert the Round17 control-family outcome into an impossibility claim.

Thank you for your consideration.
