# Journal Criteria

## Target journal
Nature Physics

## Verified venue notes
- Nature Physics states that it publishes research of the highest quality and significance across physics and offers one primary-research format, the Article.
- The journal explicitly serves a broad physics readership, so the paper must be legible beyond the narrowest Majorana-specialist audience.
- Scope clearly covers condensed-matter physics, electronics/device physics, and quantum physics/quantum technologies.

## Practical standard for this project
To be competitive as a theory-heavy Nature Physics article, this paper likely needs all of the following:
- an important and broadly legible field-level question
- a crisp conceptual advance, not only a larger catalogue of simulations
- experimentally actionable predictions with a realistic protocol window
- a result that reorganizes the discussion in the field, not just one more candidate indicator
- figures that teach quickly and can be remembered after one read

## Immediate venue-specific rules for this project
1. The main claim must be stateable in one sentence without a list of five observables.
2. The paper cannot rely on "universal diagnosis" language unless at least three materially different false-positive mechanisms are benchmarked under one protocol.
3. The paper must explain to a broad physics reader why existing zero-bias-peak evidence remains ambiguous and exactly what new diagnostic layer resolves that ambiguity.
4. Any arbitrary score function should stay out of the main text unless its physical meaning is obvious and non-ad hoc.
