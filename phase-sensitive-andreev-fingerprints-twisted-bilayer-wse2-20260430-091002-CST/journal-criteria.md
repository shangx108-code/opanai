# Journal Criteria

## Target Journal

Physical Review Letters

## Current PRL Line

This project should read as a method-and-criterion theory letter, not as a broad phenomenology paper and not as a premature topological-claim paper.

## Must-Have Conditions

1. One sharp central claim: phase-sensitive Andreev diagnostics separate the inner superconducting gap from broader correlated features and disfavor ordinary same-sign s-wave.
2. A compact but reproducible computational framework with clear decision metrics.
3. At least one coupled exclusion argument where ordinary s-wave fails across multiple constraints, not just a single curve fit.
4. Clear claim boundaries: first paper stops at unconventional family unless stronger evidence truly exists.

## Current Risks

1. No evidence-grade Andreev calculation yet.
2. No explicit inner-gap selectivity metric yet.
3. No failure map for ordinary s-wave yet.
4. No robustness package yet.

## Acceptance Gate For Internal Use

Do not claim the project is above 80% acceptance probability until all four conditions below are true:

1. Inner-gap selectivity is demonstrated with saved quantitative outputs.
2. Ordinary same-sign s-wave fails under a multi-constraint test.
3. Robustness to broadening, coarse-graining, and moderate parameter variation is documented.
4. Manuscript logic is compressed into a PRL-grade figure sequence with no evidence gaps.
