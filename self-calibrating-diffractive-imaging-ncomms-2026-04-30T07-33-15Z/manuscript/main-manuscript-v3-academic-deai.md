# A phase-only diffractive frontend for hybrid self-calibrated imaging through dynamic aberrations

## Status and claim boundary

- Target journal line: Nature Communications
- This version is an academic-deai reorganization of the manuscript around the currently executed evidence package.
- The supported claim is a hybrid optical-digital restoration gain under dynamic aberrations.
- The manuscript does not support a claim of fully optical reconstruction, benchmark-root natural-image validation, or broad hardware robustness.

## Abstract

Dynamic aberrations make coherent imaging a sample-dependent inverse problem because the forward operator changes while the instantaneous aberration state is unavailable at measurement time. We test whether a fixed phase-only diffractive frontend can encode calibration-relevant information before digital reconstruction. The implemented system is hybrid rather than fully optical: a phase-only diffractive stack is followed by intensity detection and a fixed-capacity ridge reconstruction stage. Under a pre-registered dual-ledger benchmark that separates held-out aberration realizations from held-out object-family shift, `phase_only_stack` achieved the largest pooled mean PSNR gain over the fixed baseline, with an aggregate gain of `4.057 dB` (95% CI, `3.931-4.183`). The gain was strongly regime-dependent. Performance was large on held-out aberrations and modest on held-out object-family shift, where the mean gain remained positive at `0.521 dB` and the better-than-fixed fraction was `0.5573`, but `reference_psf_deconvolution` remained stronger. Public-open-data reruns on frozen `Kodak-PCD0992` and `UCID-1338` subsets further showed that transfer beyond the synthetic ledgers is dataset-dependent: mixed training substantially reduced the original failure, `UCID` became reproducibly positive, and `Kodak-PCD0992` remained mildly negative. Tolerance analysis identified phase-mask-specific errors as the dominant engineering vulnerability. Robust-mask training increased the clean-margin performance and repaired several first-order perturbation cases, but linked scans over phase noise, layer misalignment, and wavelength drift did not widen the positive region beyond the clean `UCID` branch. A literature-bound device-prior package also shows that the current linked tolerance variables map more naturally to stacked metasurface hardware than to a single-plane SLM panel. The supported conclusion is therefore bounded: a physically constrained diffractive frontend can improve average restoration within a hybrid pipeline, but its natural-image transfer and hardware-facing interpretation remain conditional.

## Introduction

Dynamic aberrations, drift, and scattering convert coherent imaging into an inverse problem whose forward operator varies from sample to sample. In most practical settings, the degradation state is not directly known when a measurement is acquired. Existing solutions can compensate for this variability, but they often rely on active wavefront sensing, adaptive optical hardware, iterative correction, or substantial digital post-processing. A narrower and physically useful question is whether a fixed optical frontend can encode information about the current aberration state before the signal reaches the electronic reconstruction stage.

Diffractive optical processors are appealing in this setting because they impose structure at the measurement stage instead of correcting the field only after detection. Even so, many diffractive imaging studies are optimized for fixed or distribution-averaged forward models rather than for sample-specific optical self-calibration. The relevant test here is therefore not complete optical autonomy. It is whether a fixed phase-only diffractive frontend improves downstream restoration under a protocol that distinguishes generalization across aberration realizations from generalization across object families.

We study that question with a pre-registered dual-ledger benchmark composed of `same_family_heldout_aberration` and `new_family_heldout_object_family`. This split matters because a method can generalize well to unseen aberrations while still depending strongly on the object statistics seen during training. The current evidence supports a disciplined conclusion. The phase-only frontend provides the best overall mean gain in the pooled benchmark, but that advantage is carried mainly by the held-out-aberration ledger. The held-out-object-family ledger remains harder, and the public-data reruns show that transfer to natural-image structure is heterogeneous rather than uniformly positive.

## Methods

### Hybrid phase-only frontend

The system consists of a phase-only diffractive frontend, intensity detection, and a fixed-capacity digital ridge reconstruction stage. We therefore treat the method as a hybrid optical-digital pipeline throughout the manuscript. In the unified comparison, `phase_only_stack` uses `5` diffractive layers with `10` phase-basis coefficients per layer, for `50` optical trainable parameters, followed by a ridge regressor acting on the detected low-resolution features.

### Dual-ledger evaluation protocol

All benchmark comparisons followed the same dual-ledger protocol. `same_family_heldout_aberration` holds out aberration realizations while preserving the training object-family distribution, whereas `new_family_heldout_object_family` withholds entire object families from training. All methods use the same seeds, preprocessing, aberration schedules, low-resolution target size, and aggregation pipeline. No cases were removed after evaluation, and seed-level and sample-level outputs remain in the active project space.

### Comparator set and fairness boundary

We compared `phase_only_stack`, `reference_psf_deconvolution`, `parameter_matched_digital_surrogate`, `spectral_frontend`, and `trainable_surrogate_ridge` under the same protocol. `reference_psf_deconvolution` should be interpreted as a reference-guided classical comparator because it uses explicit test-time PSF information. `parameter_matched_digital_surrogate` closes the strongest remaining digital-capacity fairness gap by matching the ridge-stage parameter count of `phase_only_stack` without introducing optical trainable parameters.

### Tolerance-variable interpretation boundary

The tolerance package should not be described as hardware-native in the same way for every candidate platform. A literature-bound prior package in `results/tolerance_device_priors/` distinguishes two device classes. For a single-plane reflective LCoS SLM, `phase_noise_sigma_rad` has a direct literature anchor, but `shift_sigma_px` and `rotation_sigma_deg` are not fabrication-native panel variables and `wavelength_drift_fraction` is better interpreted as a calibration or source term. For a stacked dielectric metasurface, `phase_noise_sigma_rad`, `shift_sigma_px`, and `wavelength_drift_fraction` all admit direct literature pushforward interpretations, while `rotation_sigma_deg` remains only partially bound and still needs device-specific overlay metrology. The current linked phase-noise plus misalignment plus wavelength-drift scan therefore aligns more naturally with a stacked metasurface interpretation than with a single-plane SLM fabrication claim.

### Statistical aggregation

The unified benchmark uses `10` seeds (`0-9`). Within each seed, performance is aggregated inside each ledger and then averaged across ledgers so that the overall score is not dominated by ledger size. The primary statistic in the main text is mean PSNR gain over the fixed baseline. We also report the better-than-fixed fraction to expose negative tails that are hidden by mean gain alone.

## Results

### The phase-only frontend provides the best average dual-ledger trade-off under the registered benchmark

Under the shared 10-seed benchmark, `phase_only_stack` achieved the largest overall mean PSNR gain over the fixed baseline among the tested methods, with an aggregate gain of `4.057 dB` (95% CI, `3.931-4.183`). The corresponding pooled gains were `2.706 dB` for `reference_psf_deconvolution`, `2.393 dB` for `parameter_matched_digital_surrogate`, `1.891 dB` for `spectral_frontend`, and `1.380 dB` for `trainable_surrogate_ridge`. The appropriate reading is not uniform superiority across all regimes, but the strongest average dual-ledger trade-off under the registered aggregation rule.

This pooled ranking should be read together with the negative-tail metric. The better-than-fixed fraction of `phase_only_stack` was `0.7786`, whereas `reference_psf_deconvolution` reached `1.0`. The current evidence therefore supports a narrower claim: the phase-only frontend provides the best average benchmark trade-off, but it does not dominate every robustness metric.

### The main gain comes from held-out aberration generalization, whereas object-family shift remains the harder regime

Ledger-resolved analysis shows a clear asymmetry. On `same_family_heldout_aberration`, `phase_only_stack` achieved a mean PSNR gain of `7.5931 dB` with a better-than-fixed fraction of `1.0`. On `new_family_heldout_object_family`, the mean gain fell to `0.5211 dB`, with a better-than-fixed fraction of `0.5573`. The principal difficulty of the current system is therefore not unseen aberrations by themselves, but transfer across previously unseen object families.

This distinction matters for claim discipline. The positive mean gain on `new_family_heldout_object_family` argues against a purely memorization-based explanation, yet the effect size is much smaller than in the aberration-held-out ledger. The manuscript should therefore describe this result as a modest but positive average benefit under object-family shift, not as a solved generalization problem.

### The strongest classical comparator remains more effective in the hardest shift regime

The object-family ledger also shows where the proposed method is not strongest. Although `phase_only_stack` ranks first in the pooled benchmark, `reference_psf_deconvolution` achieved a larger mean PSNR gain than `phase_only_stack` on `new_family_heldout_object_family` (`2.5108 dB` versus `0.5211 dB`). `trainable_surrogate_ridge` was slightly negative on average (`-0.1387 dB`). This pattern narrows the main conclusion further: the current phase-only frontend gives the best overall average trade-off, but explicit test-time reference information remains more effective in the hardest shift regime.

### Public Kodak/UCID reruns show that natural-image transfer is real but strongly dataset-dependent

To probe realism beyond the synthetic ledgers, we evaluated the pipeline on staged public-open-data subsets from `Kodak-PCD0992` and `UCID-1338`. These are real public-data reruns with frozen manifests and reproducibility hashes, but they are not benchmark-root `ImageNet-1k` or `COCO` evaluations. Under the original synthetic-only training regime, the pipeline failed strongly on both datasets, with mean PSNR gains over fixed of `-16.1404 dB` for `Kodak-PCD0992` and `-12.1478 dB` for `UCID`. The original model therefore did not transfer to natural-image statistics in a useful way.

After mixed training that incorporated public natural images into the training distribution, the failure was reduced sharply but not uniformly repaired. In the first rerun, the mean PSNR gain over fixed reached `-1.1311 dB` on `Kodak-PCD0992` and `+1.3828 dB` on `UCID`. We then expanded the evaluation to `5` seeds with `12` held-out cases per seed. The resulting means remained `-1.0544 ± 0.1434 dB` for `Kodak-PCD0992` and `+1.7261 ± 0.2319 dB` for `UCID` (95% CI half-width), with mean better-than-fixed fractions of `0.1403` and `0.8153`, respectively. These data support a narrower inference than broad natural-image generalization: mixed training materially reduces the transfer failure, but the recovery is strongly dataset-dependent rather than universally positive.

### Mild shared perturbations are not the main engineering problem; mask-specific perturbations are

We next examined tolerance in the mixed synthetic plus public-natural regime. Across common perturbations, `phase_only_stack` remained positive on `UCID` but negative on `Kodak-PCD0992`, even at clean reference and under `±5%` propagation-distance error. At clean reference, the mean gains were `+1.6409 dB` on `UCID` and `-1.1084 dB` on `Kodak-PCD0992`. Under reference-channel intensity noise of `0.01` and `0.02`, `UCID` remained positive at `+1.6014 dB` and `+1.4643 dB`, whereas `Kodak-PCD0992` remained negative at `-1.1789 dB` and `-1.3759 dB`. Mild shared perturbations therefore do not erase the gain where it exists, but they also do not rescue the harder Kodak subset.

The dominant vulnerability is instead tied to the learned phase mask. In the first tolerance pass, `1 px` and `2 px` lateral shift, as well as `3`-bit and `4`-bit quantization, drove `phase_only_stack` strongly negative on both public datasets. For example, `1 px` shift reduced the mean PSNR gain to `-15.6732 dB` on `Kodak-PCD0992` and `-11.4353 dB` on `UCID`, whereas `3`-bit quantization yielded `-13.9654 dB` and `-9.6618 dB`, respectively. The reviewer-facing engineering concern is therefore not mild shared-input perturbation, but mask-specific fabrication and alignment sensitivity.

### Robust-mask training raises clean margin and repairs several first-order failures, but it does not widen the linked tolerance window

To test whether this sensitivity is partly learnable, we trained the phase-only model with in-loop exposure to unperturbed masks, `4`-bit quantization, `3`-bit quantization, and `1 px` lateral shift. This robust-mask intervention improved clean-reference performance to `+2.5993 dB` on `UCID` and `-0.2182 dB` on `Kodak-PCD0992`, compared with `+1.6409 dB` and `-1.1084 dB` in the unmitigated tolerance package. In that limited sense, the mitigation raises the clean operating margin where a positive clean point already exists and narrows the clean-point deficit on the harder Kodak subset.

The same intervention also repairs several first-order perturbation cases, but asymmetrically. On `UCID`, the mean PSNR gain became `+2.3329 dB` under `1 px` lateral shift, `+2.4140 dB` under `3`-bit quantization, and `+2.5972 dB` under `4`-bit quantization. On `Kodak-PCD0992`, the corresponding gains improved from strongly negative values to only slightly negative values: `-0.3333 dB`, `-0.3274 dB`, and `-0.2536 dB`, respectively. Even so, `2 px` lateral shift remained strongly negative on both datasets, with gains of `-16.5628 dB` on `Kodak-PCD0992` and `-12.3464 dB` on `UCID`.

The stricter test is the linked fabrication-style scan over phase noise, layer misalignment, and wavelength drift. In the unmitigated stack, `UCID` retained a positive region only on the clean-mask branch, from `+1.6409 ± 0.3407 dB` at `0%` drift to `+1.6255 ± 0.3339 dB` at `2%` drift, while adding `0.05 rad` phase noise or mild misalignment (`0.25 px / 0.5 deg`) already drove the gain negative. `Kodak-PCD0992` had no passing region even at the clean point (`-1.1084 ± 0.1963 dB`). After robust-mask training, the clean `UCID` branch improved to `+2.5993 ± 0.0812 dB` at `0%` drift and `+2.5192 ± 0.1570 dB` at `2%` drift, but the pass set still did not expand beyond that branch: `UCID` remained negative at `-4.3309 ± 1.7409 dB` for phase noise only and `-7.4303 ± 0.5466 dB` for mild misalignment only, while `Kodak-PCD0992` stayed negative at the clean point. The robust-mask result should therefore be described as improving clean margin and repairing selected first-order cases, not as establishing broad hardware robustness.

### A first second-generation joint-perturbation objective remains a meaningful negative result

We also tested whether explicitly injecting linked perturbation variants during training could open a broader fabrication-tolerance window. The second-generation objective included clean, joint mild (`0.05 rad`, `0.25 px`, `0.5 deg`, `0.5%`), and joint moderate (`0.10 rad`, `0.50 px`, `1.0 deg`, `1.0%`) perturbation states during optimization. This first-pass objective did not widen the pass region. On `UCID`, the clean branch remained positive at `+2.3951 ± 0.2853 dB` at `0%` drift and `+2.3037 ± 0.3357 dB` at `2%` drift, but phase noise only (`0.05 rad`) still produced `-4.7289 ± 1.9562 dB`, and mild misalignment only (`0.25 px / 0.5 deg`) produced `-8.3056 ± 2.1266 dB`. `Kodak-PCD0992` also remained negative at the clean point (`-0.4409 ± 0.1989 dB`).

This result should be retained rather than hidden. Relative to the first robust-mask strategy, the second-generation objective preserves only the same clean `UCID` branch and underperforms the robust-mask clean point. It is therefore a valid negative result: joint perturbation exposure in its current form does not open the linked fabrication-tolerance window.

## Discussion

Taken together, the evidence supports a narrow but coherent story. Under the registered dual-ledger benchmark, the phase-only diffractive frontend yields the strongest average gain among the tested methods, but that advantage is carried mainly by held-out-aberration generalization. The object-family-shift result remains positive on average yet modest in size, so the manuscript should frame this as a hybrid optical-digital gain under dynamic aberrations rather than as universal optical self-calibration.

The public Kodak/UCID reruns sharpen the same boundary. The strongly negative synthetic-only transfer shows that the original model does not automatically generalize to natural-image structure. Mixed training shows that part of the failure is a distribution-alignment problem rather than a complete incompatibility with natural images. At the same time, the real public-data branch forces a stricter conclusion than the earlier proxy-natural story: `UCID` becomes reproducibly positive, but `Kodak-PCD0992` remains mildly negative. The correct interpretation is therefore dataset-dependent partial recovery, not broad natural-image generalization.

The tolerance package sharpens the hardware-facing claim in the same way. Wavelength drift up to `2%` is relatively mild when the mask is otherwise clean, whereas phase noise and layer misalignment dominate the failure boundary. Robust-mask training repairs several first-order perturbation failures on `UCID` and materially narrows the clean-point deficit on `Kodak-PCD0992`, but it does not widen the linked pass region beyond clean `UCID`. The first second-generation joint-training objective also fails to change that boundary. Finally, the literature-bound prior package clarifies that the present linked tolerance variables map more cleanly onto stacked metasurface hardware than onto a single-plane SLM panel. The current engineering conclusion is therefore constrained twice: by the executed robustness results and by the physical interpretation of the simulated variables themselves.

## Limitations

Several limitations should remain explicit. First, the present implementation is a hybrid optical-digital pipeline because the optical frontend is followed by a digital reconstruction stage. Second, the strongest generalization result lies in the held-out-aberration ledger rather than the held-out-object-family ledger. Third, the natural-image analyses currently rest on staged public `Kodak-PCD0992` and `UCID-1338` subsets rather than on benchmark-root `ImageNet-1k` or `COCO`, and the outcomes are strongly dataset-dependent even within this real public-data branch. Fourth, the tolerance package now includes unmitigated, robust-mask, and second-generation linked scans, but none of these results supports a broad fabrication-tolerance window. Fifth, the device-interpretation package does not yet supply a fully device-specific degree-scale prior for rotational overlay error.

## Conclusion

The present evidence package supports a restrained Nature Communications-level conclusion. A phase-only diffractive optical frontend can improve average image restoration under dynamic aberrations within a hybrid optical-digital pipeline, and this benefit survives a registered object-family-shift benchmark while extending only selectively to real public natural-image data. The same evidence shows that natural-image transfer is strongly dataset-dependent and that mask-specific engineering sensitivity remains a central limitation. Robust-mask training raises clean operating margin and repairs several first-order failures, but linked fabrication-style scans and a first second-generation joint-training objective both show that the positive region does not expand beyond the clean `UCID` branch. The most defensible claim is therefore not that self-calibrated optical imaging has been solved in full, but that a physically constrained diffractive frontend can deliver measurable average benefit under dynamic aberrations when its evidence boundary and hardware interpretation are stated precisely.
