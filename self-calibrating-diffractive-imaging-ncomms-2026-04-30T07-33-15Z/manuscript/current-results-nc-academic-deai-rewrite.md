## Revised Version

### Results

#### The phase-only frontend gives the strongest overall mean gain under the registered dual-ledger benchmark

We first assessed the hybrid optical-digital system under a pre-registered dual-ledger protocol that separates generalization across aberration realizations from generalization across object families. Under the shared 10-seed benchmark, `phase_only_stack` achieved the largest overall mean PSNR gain over the fixed baseline among the tested methods, with an aggregate gain of `4.057 dB` (95% CI, `3.931-4.183`). The corresponding gains were `2.706 dB` for `reference_psf_deconvolution`, `2.393 dB` for `parameter_matched_digital_surrogate`, `1.891 dB` for `spectral_frontend`, and `1.380 dB` for `trainable_surrogate_ridge`. This ranking indicates that the diffractive frontend contributes a measurable average advantage when both ledgers are pooled using the same aggregation rule, even after matching the digital parameter count of the downstream reconstruction stage.

This overall advantage, however, should not be interpreted as uniform dominance across all robustness metrics. The better-than-fixed fraction of `phase_only_stack` was `0.7786`, whereas `reference_psf_deconvolution` reached `1.0`. The present evidence therefore supports a more specific conclusion: the phase-only frontend yields the strongest average gain in the registered benchmark, but it still exhibits a non-negligible negative tail that must be accounted for explicitly.

#### The gain is large for held-out aberrations but modest for held-out object-family shift

The ledger-resolved analysis shows a clear asymmetry between the two generalization regimes. On `same_family_heldout_aberration`, `phase_only_stack` achieved a mean PSNR gain of `7.593 dB` with a better-than-fixed fraction of `1.0`. By contrast, on `new_family_heldout_object_family`, the gain decreased to `0.521 dB`, with a better-than-fixed fraction of `0.5573`. The main difficulty of the current system is therefore not aberration-only generalization, but transfer across previously unseen object families.

This distinction is important for claim calibration. The positive mean gain on `new_family_heldout_object_family` argues against a purely memorization-based explanation, yet the effect size is substantially smaller than in the same-family ledger. A reviewer-safe interpretation is therefore that the phase-only frontend retains a modest average benefit under object-family shift, rather than that it has already solved robust object-level generalization.

#### The strongest baseline remains competitive in the harder shift regime

The object-family ledger also clarifies where the present method is and is not strongest. Although `phase_only_stack` ranks first in the overall benchmark, it is not the best method on every sub-regime. In particular, `reference_psf_deconvolution` achieved a larger mean PSNR gain than `phase_only_stack` on `new_family_heldout_object_family` (`2.511 dB` versus `0.521 dB`), while `trainable_surrogate_ridge` became slightly negative on average (`-0.1387 dB`). This pattern narrows the main conclusion: the current phase-only frontend provides the best overall tradeoff across the registered benchmark, but the strongest classical reference-guided baseline remains more reliable in the hardest object-family-shift ledger.

#### Public Kodak/UCID natural-image evaluation reveals strong dataset dependence

To probe realism beyond the synthetic ledgers, we next evaluated the pipeline on staged public-open-data subsets from `Kodak-PCD0992` and `UCID-1338`. These runs are real public-data evaluations with frozen manifests, but they are not benchmark-root `ImageNet` or `COCO` results. Under the original synthetic-only training regime, the pipeline failed strongly on both datasets, with mean PSNR gains over fixed of `-16.1404 dB` for `Kodak-PCD0992` and `-12.1478 dB` for `UCID`. Thus, the initial model did not transfer to natural-image statistics in any meaningful way.

After retraining under a mixed regime that incorporated public natural images into the training distribution, the failure was reduced sharply but not uniformly repaired. In a first rerun, the mean PSNR gain over fixed reached `-1.1311 dB` on `Kodak-PCD0992` and `+1.3828 dB` on `UCID`. We then thickened this evaluation to `5` seeds with `12` held-out cases per seed. The resulting mean gains remained at `-1.0544 ± 0.1434 dB` for `Kodak-PCD0992` and `+1.7261 ± 0.2319 dB` for `UCID` (95% CI half-width), with mean better-than-fixed fractions of `0.1403` and `0.8153`, respectively. These data support a narrower inference than broad natural-image generalization: the negative transfer seen in the original model is largely attributable to training-distribution mismatch, but the mixed-training recovery is strongly dataset-dependent rather than universally positive.

#### Common perturbations are tolerated, whereas mask-specific engineering errors dominate the current robustness risk

We next examined tolerance in the mixed synthetic plus public-natural regime. Across common perturbations, the phase-only frontend remained positive on `UCID` but negative on `Kodak-PCD0992`, even at clean reference and under `±5% z` propagation-distance error. At clean reference, the mean gain was `+1.6409 dB` on `UCID` but `-1.1084 dB` on `Kodak-PCD0992`. These results indicate that mild shared perturbations do not erase the recovered gain where it exists, but they also do not rescue the harder Kodak subset.

The dominant robustness liability instead arises from phase-mask-specific engineering perturbations. In the initial tolerance pass, `1 px` and `2 px` lateral shift, as well as `3`-bit and `4`-bit quantization, drove `phase_only_stack` strongly negative on both public datasets. For example, `1 px` shift reduced the mean PSNR gain to `-15.6732 dB` on `Kodak-PCD0992` and `-11.4353 dB` on `UCID`, whereas `3`-bit quantization yielded `-13.9654 dB` and `-9.6618 dB`, respectively. The main reviewer-facing hardware concern is therefore not mild shared-input perturbation, but the sensitivity of the learned mask to fabrication and alignment errors.

#### Robust-mask training repairs first-order quantization and alignment failures but not larger displacement

To test whether this engineering sensitivity is itself learnable, we performed a robust-mask mitigation pass in which the phase-only model was trained with in-loop exposure to unperturbed masks, `4`-bit quantization, `3`-bit quantization, and `1 px` lateral shift. This intervention improved clean-reference performance to `+2.5993 dB` on `UCID` and to `-0.2182 dB` on `Kodak-PCD0992`, compared with tolerance-baseline gains of `+1.6409 dB` and `-1.1084 dB`, respectively. In that limited sense, the mitigation raises clean margin where a positive clean point already exists and narrows the clean-point deficit on the harder Kodak subset.

More importantly, the same mitigation largely repaired the first-order engineering failures, but in an asymmetric way. On `UCID`, the mean PSNR gain became `+2.3329 dB` under `1 px` lateral shift, `+2.4140 dB` under `3`-bit quantization, and `+2.5972 dB` under `4`-bit quantization. On `Kodak-PCD0992`, the corresponding gains improved dramatically from large negative values to only slightly negative values: `-0.3333 dB` under `1 px` shift, `-0.3274 dB` under `3`-bit quantization, and `-0.2536 dB` under `4`-bit quantization. Nevertheless, `2 px` lateral shift remained strongly negative on both datasets, with gains of `-16.5628 dB` on `Kodak-PCD0992` and `-12.3464 dB` on `UCID`.

The stricter test is the linked fabrication-style scan over phase noise, layer misalignment, and wavelength drift. Under that scan, the same robust-mask model keeps only the clean `UCID` branch positive: the clean-point gain rises from `+1.6409 dB` to `+2.5993 dB`, and the clean branch remains positive through `2%` wavelength drift at `+2.5192 dB`. However, the pass region does not widen beyond that branch. On `UCID`, adding only `0.05 rad` phase noise drives the mean gain to `-4.3309 dB`, and adding only mild misalignment (`0.25 px` shift sigma and `0.5 deg` rotation sigma) drives it to `-7.4303 dB`; `Kodak-PCD0992` remains negative even at the clean point (`-0.2182 dB`). The robust-mask result should therefore be described as raising clean margin and repairing selected first-order cases, not as widening the linked tolerance window.

### Discussion

Taken together, the current data support a bounded but coherent conclusion. Under the registered synthetic dual-ledger benchmark, the phase-only diffractive frontend provides the strongest overall mean gain among the tested methods. That advantage is driven mainly by strong performance on held-out aberration realizations, whereas the effect under held-out object-family shift remains positive but modest. The present manuscript should therefore frame the system as a hybrid optical-digital frontend that improves average restoration under dynamic aberrations, not as a universally dominant or fully autonomous optical reconstructor.

The public Kodak/UCID natural-image analyses sharpen this interpretation. The strongly negative synthetic-only transfer shows that the original model does not generalize automatically to natural-image structure. The mixed-training rerun indicates that the optical frontend is not intrinsically incompatible with natural images, but that its usefulness depends on better alignment between training and deployment distributions. At the same time, the public-data rerun forces a stricter claim boundary than the earlier proxy branch: `UCID` becomes reproducibly positive, whereas `Kodak-PCD0992` remains mildly negative. These tests should therefore support mechanism and claim-boundary arguments about dataset-dependent transfer rather than be packaged as broad natural-image validation.

The tolerance results further separate two types of robustness. Shared perturbations such as mild reference noise and `±5%` propagation-distance error do not erase the recovered `UCID` gain, but they also do not rescue `Kodak-PCD0992`. By contrast, phase-mask quantization and lateral shift are severe engineering stressors for the current implementation. Robust-mask training shows that a substantial part of this sensitivity can be mitigated through exposure during optimization, with strong first-order repair on `UCID` and substantial but incomplete repair on `Kodak-PCD0992`, yet the linked phase-noise-plus-misalignment-plus-wavelength scan shows that the same mitigation does not widen the tolerance window beyond clean `UCID`. Fabrication and alignment tolerances therefore remain an open constraint on any stronger hardware-facing claim.

### Limitations

Several limitations should remain explicit. First, the present implementation is a hybrid optical-digital pipeline, because the optical frontend is followed by a digital reconstruction stage. Second, the strongest generalization result lies in the held-out-aberration ledger rather than the held-out-object-family ledger. Third, the natural-image analyses now rest on staged public Kodak/UCID subsets rather than on benchmark-root `ImageNet-1k` or `COCO`, and even within this real public-data branch the outcomes are strongly dataset-dependent. Fourth, the tolerance evidence now includes both first-order and linked fabrication-style scans, but the executed robustness package still supports only a constrained engineering conclusion, especially because `Kodak-PCD0992` remains negative at the clean point after robust-mask training and the linked pass region does not extend beyond clean `UCID`.

### Conclusion

The current evidence package supports a restrained `Nature Communications`-level conclusion: a phase-only diffractive optical frontend can improve average image restoration under dynamic aberrations within a hybrid optical-digital pipeline, and this benefit survives the registered object-family-shift benchmark while extending only selectively to real public natural-image data. The same data show that natural-image transfer is strongly dataset-dependent and that mask-specific engineering sensitivity remains a central limitation. Robust-mask training can raise clean operating margin and strongly repair several first-order failures on `UCID`, but the linked fabrication-style scan shows that this strategy does not widen the tolerance window beyond the clean `UCID` branch. The most defensible claim is therefore not that self-calibrated optical imaging has been solved in full, but that a physically constrained diffractive frontend can deliver measurable average benefit under dynamic aberrations when its claim boundary is defined carefully and its public-data heterogeneity is stated plainly.
