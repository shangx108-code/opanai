# Figure 5 caption package: self-calibrating-diffractive-ncomms

## Recommended Figure 5 title
Figure 5 | Passive diffractive processor comparison under a shared dynamic-aberration protocol

## Recommended caption
Figure 5 compares ordinary and pilot-assisted passive diffractive processors under one shared lightweight dynamic-aberration protocol with non-common-path and wrong-reference controls. The main supported claim is not that full self-calibration has been robustly demonstrated, but that a repeat-stable local common-path advantage over the ordinary baseline now exists in the current passive phase-only processor family and that structural wrong-reference redesign improves the stricter exclusion diagnostic under higher-repeat confirmation. In the strongest locally stable ordinary-focused window, the common-path condition exceeds the ordinary baseline by about 0.4 dB on average and remains positive across repeats. In the round17-confirmed structural window using sparse_tracker_decoy plus occupancy_guarded, the common-minus-ordinary mean is +0.638 dB with a +0.413 dB worst repeat, while the common-minus-wrong-reference mean is +0.278 dB with a +0.047 dB worst repeat. The paired risk-sample lower decile is +0.090 dB and the positive fraction is 0.964, but one risk-pair minimum remains slightly negative at -0.025 dB. Figure 5 should therefore be read as evidence for a robust ordinary-baseline advantage with substantially improved but still not fully closed wrong-reference exclusion, not as a final proof that processor-level self-calibration has been cleanly isolated.

## Caption boundary notes
- Do say: ordinary-baseline advantage is now locally repeat-stable in the current lightweight protocol.
- Do say: structural redesign makes the paired wrong-reference diagnostic mostly positive under higher-repeat confirmation, while one negative tail event remains.
- Do not say: self-calibration is conclusively demonstrated at the processor level.
- Do not say: wrong-reference exclusion is fully closed, general, or architecture-independent.
