# Journal Criteria

## Target journal
Laser & Photonics Reviews

## Verified venue notes
- Laser & Photonics Reviews publishes top-quality Reviews, original Research Articles, and Perspectives across photonics and optics, including programmable photonics, photonic neural networks, quantum photonics, silicon photonics, and nonlinear optics.
- The venue accommodates theory, experiment, and device-driven systems papers.
- The paper therefore needs strong photonics substance, practical systems relevance, and a clear device or architecture consequence. It does not need the broader field-redefining burden expected at Nature-branded journals.

## Practical standard for this project
To be competitive as a theory-and-simulation LPR research article, this paper likely needs all of the following:
- a clear photonic systems problem with immediate relevance to photonic neural hardware
- a technically precise framework or architecture with explicit physical assumptions
- benchmark evidence that changes a design decision, not only a conceptual taxonomy
- a practical route to device-level implementation or validation
- clear positioning relative to recent LPR and adjacent photonic neural-network papers

## Immediate venue-specific rules for this project
1. The abstract must open from a photonic-computing motivation and pivot quickly to the activation bottleneck.
2. The manuscript should use a direct `Here, ...` contribution sentence and keep the main claim device-facing.
3. `Quantum limit` or `quantum cost` language must remain tied to explicit accounting assumptions.
4. The paper should state useful operating windows rather than implying unconditional superiority.
5. The main text should sound like a photonics research article, not a resource-theory manifesto.
